## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Saha et al. - Project Sidewalk** [110] -- Crowdsourced sidewalk accessibility assessment using Google Street View imagery. Directly relevant to the intersection of street view imagery and accessibility assessment; a natural complement to StreetReaderAI's exploration approach.

2. **Gurari et al. (2018) - VizWiz Grand Challenge** [60] -- Visual question answering benchmark from photos taken by blind users. Establishes the paradigm of blind users asking questions about visual content that StreetReaderAI extends to streetscape imagery.

3. **Microsoft SoundScape** [90] -- 3D audio-based spatial navigation for blind and low-vision users. The primary prior art for spatial audio navigation that StreetReaderAI builds upon and extends with visual AI descriptions.

4. **Bigham et al. (2010) - VizWiz** [17] -- The foundational system for blind users asking visual questions via mobile devices. Established the real-time visual question answering paradigm that StreetReaderAI adapts to streetscape contexts.

5. **Zhao et al. (2019) - AR Visualizations for Stair Navigation** [138] -- Accessible AR for low-vision users at UIST '19. Represents the intersection of AR, navigation, and visual impairment accessibility that complements StreetReaderAI's virtual approach.
