## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Borg, Lantz, Gulliksen (2015) [ref 23]** — "Accessibility to electronic communication for people with cognitive disabilities: A systematic search and review of empirical evidence." *Universal Access in the Information Society* — Direct predecessor review that Gartland et al. expand on; essential for understanding the evolution of cognitive accessibility evidence. Highly relevant for collection.

2. **Karreman, van der Geest, Buursink (2007) [ref 40]** — "Accessible Website Content Guidelines for Users with Intellectual Disabilities." *J. Appl. Res. Intellect. Disabil.* — Provides empirical evidence for easy-to-read design principles; one of the most-cited positive intervention results in the corpus.

3. **Lazar et al. (2018) [ref 60]** — "Co-Design Process of a Smart Phone App to Help People With Down Syndrome Manage Their Nutritional Habits." *J. Usability Stud.* — Best methodological example of participatory co-design with PwCDs; directly demonstrates the best-practices model from Figure 5.

4. **Ophoff, Johnson, Renaud (2021) [ref 56]** — "Cognitive function vs accessible authentication: Insights from dyslexia research." W4A 2021 — Direct evidence for WCAG 3.3.7 (Accessible Authentication) from dyslexia perspective; CAPTCHA and password barriers quantified.

5. **Mack et al. (2021) [ref 25]** — Already in collection as Mack_2021_AccessibilityResearchSurvey — cited here for its finding that PwCDs are underrepresented in CHI/ASSETS accessibility research, which this systematic review's corpus composition confirms.
