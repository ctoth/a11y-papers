## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Gajos, Wobbrock, and Weld (2008)** — "Improving the performance of motor-impaired users with automatically-generated, ability-based interfaces" (CHI '08) — SUPPLE's core evaluation paper showing 28% speed and 73% accuracy gains; the deepest empirical demonstration of ability-based design in practice.

2. **Wobbrock, Fogarty, Liu, Kimuro, and Harada (2009)** — "The Angle Mouse" (CHI '09) — Target-agnostic angular deviation gain adjustment; demonstrates ability-based motor adaptation without OS/application cooperation.

3. **Hurst, Hudson, Mankoff, and Trewin (2008a)** — "Automatically detecting pointing performance" (IUI '08) — Ability measurement without explicit test battery; critical for Principle 5 (Performance) in real-world deployment.

4. **Kane, Bigham, and Wobbrock (2008a)** — "Slide Rule" (ASSETS '08) — Blind users on touch screens via multi-touch gestures; demonstrates ability-based design leveraging existing spatial navigation abilities of blind users.

5. **Froehlich, Wobbrock, and Kane (2007)** — "Barrier Pointing" (ASSETS '07) — Mobile touch accessibility via physical screen bezels; demonstrates Principle 6 (Context) — using physical environment as accessibility resource.
