Giudice et al. (2020) conduct a within-subjects behavioral experiment with eight blind participants comparing a vibro-audio map (VAM) -- a touchscreen-based digital interactive map using vibrotactile and auditory cues on a commercial Samsung tablet -- against a traditional embossed hardcopy tactile map overlay (HTM), using an environmental transfer paradigm where participants learned building floor plans from maps and then navigated corresponding real physical spaces from memory. Bayesian equivalence testing (HDI + ROPE) found highly similar wayfinding accuracy (HIM 78%, DIM 75%) and route efficiency (HIM 79%, DIM 67%) between conditions, with the DIM requiring more learning time (364s vs. 194s), demonstrating that raised-line tactile stimuli are not necessary for building accurate cognitive maps and that affordable touchscreen devices can replace expensive specialized tactile map production. For this collection, the paper provides the strongest behavioral evidence that commercial touchscreen-based accessible maps achieve equivalent spatial learning outcomes to traditional tactile maps, validating low-cost DIM approaches for the visual impairment research area and directly connecting to Biggs_2025_MapEquivalentPurpose's equivalence evaluation framework.
