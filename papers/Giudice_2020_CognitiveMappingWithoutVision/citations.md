## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Ducasse, Brock, and Jouffrais (2018)** -- "Accessible interactive maps for visually impaired users" -- The most comprehensive taxonomy of accessible map types (HIMs vs. DIMs), directly relevant for categorizing and evaluating accessible map approaches.

2. **Loomis, Klatzky, and Giudice (2013)** -- "Representing 3D space in working memory: spatial images from vision, touch, hearing, and language" -- The theoretical foundation for the "amodal spatial representation" hypothesis that underpins why touchscreen maps can work as well as tactile maps; foundational for understanding cross-modal spatial cognition.

3. **Brayda et al. (2018)** -- "Updated tactile feedback with a pin array matrix helps blind people to reduce self-location errors" -- A comparison study using refreshable pin-array displays, providing a different technology comparison point for spatial learning by blind users.

4. **Espinosa et al. (1998)** -- "Comparing methods for introducing blind and visually impaired people to unfamiliar urban environments" -- Foundational environmental transfer methodology study, directly relevant to evaluation methods for accessible navigation tools.

5. **Simonnet et al. (2019)** -- "Comparing interaction techniques to help blind people explore maps on small tactile devices" -- Most recent comparison of map exploration techniques on small touchscreens, extending the VAM approach to watch-sized displays.
