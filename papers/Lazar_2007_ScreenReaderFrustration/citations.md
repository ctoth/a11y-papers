## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Ceaparu et al. (2004)** — "Determining Causes and Severity of End-User Frustration" — IJHCI 17:333–356 — This is the direct methodological predecessor; the time diary approach and Percent Time Lost formula used in this paper were validated here. Essential for understanding the measurement method.

2. **Lazar, Jones, & Shneiderman (2006)** — "Workplace user frustration with computers" — Behaviour and Information Technology — The parallel study with sighted workplace users, providing the comparison baseline for the 42.7% time-lost statistic and the contrasting response behaviors (sighted users more likely to reboot).

3. **Hackett, Parmento & Zeng (2004)** — "Accessibility of Internet Websites through time" — ACM ASSETS 2004 — Longitudinal study of whether web accessibility improves over time; relevant to whether the barriers found in Lazar 2007 are getting better or worse.

4. **Zajicek, Powell & Reeves (1998)** — "A Web navigation tool for the blind" — ACM ASSETS 1998 — BrookesTalk: integrated screen reader + browser providing more navigational information; cited as an example of enhanced screen reader design, relevant to screen reader architecture.

5. **Shneiderman (2000)** — "Universal usability" — CACM 43:84–91 — The theoretical framework motivating this study; defines universal usability as ensuring all users can actually use technology, not just technically access it.
