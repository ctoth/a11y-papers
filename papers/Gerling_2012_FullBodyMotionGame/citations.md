## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Rice et al. (2011) [ref 23]** — Direct predecessor: gesture-based games with 36 older adults on large-screen display, specifically suggested hands-free gesture gaming for institutionalized older adults as future work. Gerling et al. directly answers this call.

- **Norton, Wingrave, and LaViola (2010) [ref 21]** — Guidelines for full-body video game interfaces from a younger-audience perspective; Gerling's guidelines explicitly extend and specialize Norton's for older adults. Relevant for comparing population-general vs. age-specific design guidelines.

- **Flores et al. (2008) [ref 10]** — Game design for motor rehabilitation; recommends ROM-appropriate movements and skill-level adaptation. Gerling's calibration system implements these recommendations in a non-clinical entertainment context.

- **Watson, Clark, and Tellegen (1988) [ref 28]** — PANAS scale validation paper. The measurement instrument used to demonstrate mood improvement — foundational for any study using PANAS as an emotional well-being outcome measure.

- **Ketcham and Stelmach (2001) [ref 18]** — Authoritative source on age-related motor control declines (muscle mass loss, balance, gait, motor learning difficulties). Provides the physiological grounding for why Gerling's gesture set and guidelines are structured the way they are.
