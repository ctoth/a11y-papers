## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Ref [25] — WebAIM Million (2021)**: The most-cited large-scale comparison study. Uses WAVE tool (not ACT-Rules), finds 51.4 errors/page vs. this paper's 30. Freely available; essential for methodology comparison and ARIA-specific statistics (WebAIM reports ARIA usage correlated with 68% more errors).

2. **Ref [15] — Duarte et al. (2016) W4A**: Direct predecessor establishing that web development technology affects accessibility. Evaluated 1,669 pages; this paper scales that approach to 2.9M pages. Directly relevant to ARIA since CMSes and frameworks mediate ARIA implementation quality.

3. **Ref [36] — Martins & Duarte (2022) UAIS**: Same authors' prior paper comparing 11 accessibility metrics on the same dataset. Establishes why A3 was chosen for this study. Important for understanding metric validity.

4. **Ref [34] — Fernandes et al. (2012) W4A**: Earlier paper explicitly evaluating accessibility of Rich Internet Applications (RIAs) — directly evaluates ARIA-dependent dynamic web content accessibility. The gap this paper fills at scale.

5. **Ref [27] — Antonelli et al. (2018) DSAI**: Survey of Brazilian web developer awareness of accessibility. Cited as evidence that two-thirds of developers don't consider accessibility in their projects — provides the human/organizational context for why the automated evaluation finds so many errors.
