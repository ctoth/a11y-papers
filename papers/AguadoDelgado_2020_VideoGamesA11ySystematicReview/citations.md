## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Yuan, Folmer, Harris (2011)** [ref 52] — "Game accessibility: a survey." Univ. Access Inf. Soc. 10, 81–100 — **Already in collection as Yuan_2011_GameAccessibilitySurvey.** The foundational framework paper for game accessibility this review builds upon.

2. **Barlet and Spohn (2012)** [ref 4] — "A practical guide to game accessibility." The Ablegamers Foundation — The primary practitioner guideline document for game developers; the closest analog to WCAG for games. Not yet in collection.

3. **Porter and Kientz (2013)** [ref 43] — "An empirical study of issues and barriers to mainstream video game accessibility." ACM SIGACCESS 2013 — Empirical barrier study answering 4/6 research questions; the most cited barrier-characterization paper in this corpus. Not yet in collection.

4. **Grammenos, Savidis, Stephanidis (2007)** [ref 22] — "Unified design of universally accessible games." Int Conf Univers Access Hum Comput Interact 4556:607–616 — The unified/universal design approach to game accessibility; cited across multiple high-value studies. Not yet in collection.

5. **Gerling, Livingston, Nacke et al. (2012)** [ref 16] — "Full-body motion-based game interaction for older adults." SIGCHI 2012 — Motor accessibility through NUI/Kinect; cited in S03 and S26; heavily cross-referenced. Not yet in collection.
