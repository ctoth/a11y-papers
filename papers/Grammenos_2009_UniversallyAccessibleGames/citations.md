## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Savidis and Stephanidis (2004)** — "Unified user interface design: Designing universally accessible interactions." *Int. J. Interacting with Computers 16*, 2, 243–270. — The formal design methodology foundation underlying all UA-Games work; essential for understanding the polymorphic specialization and runtime polymorphism concepts used throughout the paper.

2. **Grammenos et al. (2007)** — "Unified design of universally accessible games." UAHCI conference, Springer, Berlin, 607–616. — Companion paper that elaborates each step of the design process in detail; referenced repeatedly in the paper as the full methodology description.

3. **Ntoa et al. (2004)** — "FastScanner: An accessibility tool for motor impaired users." ICCHP 2004. — The scanning/switching accessibility technology directly employed in UA-Chess; relevant for motor impairment and assistive technology topic areas.

4. **Bierre et al. (2005)** — "Game not over: Accessibility issues in video games." UAHCI 2005. — A comprehensive overview of game accessibility problems and solutions across disability types, contemporaneous with this work; useful survey predecessor.

5. **IGDA GA-SIG (2004)** — White paper on game accessibility — The industry guidelines document that directly informed the 21 levels of Game Over!; primary industry-side accessibility standards for games at the time.
