## Key Citations for Follow-up

**[27] Yuan, Folmer, Harris 2010** — "Game accessibility: a survey" — Already in collection as Yuan_2011_GameAccessibilitySurvey. Porter explicitly positions this paper as the empirical complement to Yuan's theoretical framework, and critiques it for lacking empirical grounding.

**[4] Bigham, Ladner, Borodin 2011** — "The design of human-powered access technology" (ASSETS '11) — Porter uses this to frame the social assistance workaround (relying on friends/PCAs to help play games) as a legitimate design space. Not yet in collection; relevant to assistive technology and independence.

**[14] Miesenberger et al. 2008** — "More Than Just a Game: Accessibility in Computer Games" — Framing paper Porter cites for the social participation argument ("it is about [allowing them to take] part in a societal phenomenon of growing importance"). Relevant for universal design / social inclusion angle.

**[21] Trewin et al. 2009** — "Exploring Visual and Motor Accessibility in Navigating a Virtual World" (TACCESS) — The most rigorous prior empirical work on game accessibility; Porter's study extends beyond it. Motor + visual accessibility in a virtual world.

**[3] Bierre et al. 2004** — IGDA GA SIG white paper "Accessibility in Games: Motivations and Approaches" — The guidelines document used in Porter's priming activity (Table 1). Foundational community document for game accessibility guidelines; historically important as the first organized industry-side accessibility framework.
