## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Ref 19 — Newell (1995) "Extra-ordinary Human Computer Operation"**: Introduces the multi-dimensional functionality-space model of the user and the ordinary/extraordinary framing. Foundational to USID; should be in this collection as the theoretical predecessor.

- **Ref 22 — Newell & Cairns (1993) "Designing for extra-ordinary users"**: The "Ergonomics in Design" paper that elaborates the ordinary/extraordinary model. Directly precedes this paper and coins terminology used throughout the accessibility literature.

- **Ref 9 — Hypponen (1999) "Handbook on Inclusive Design for Telematics Applications"**: The EU INCLUDE project methodology that this paper explicitly responds to and critiques. Understanding Hypponen clarifies exactly what USID is a counterproposal to.

- **Ref 4 — Arnott & Javed (1992) "Probabilistic Character Disambiguation for Reduced Keyboards"**: Cited as the rehabilitation-engineering origin of disambiguation techniques later adopted by commercial predictive text (T9). Relevant for AT → mainstream technology transfer research.

- **Ref 29 — Sleeman (1998) "Disability's New Paradigm"**: The PAR-oriented position this paper critiques. Reading both gives a full picture of the 1990s debate over disabled user participation in research methodology.
