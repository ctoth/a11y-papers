## Key Citations for Follow-up

**1. Wobbrock et al. 2011 — Ability-Based Design (ref #158)**
Kane, Gajos, Harada, Froehlich. *ACM Transactions on Accessible Computing* 3, 3: 1–27. https://doi.org/10.1145/1952383.1952384
Foundational framework proposing that technology should adapt to users' existing abilities rather than requiring users to adapt. The canonical design philosophy paper for the accessibility field — essential reading for any work on adaptive or assistive interfaces.

**2. Mankoff, Hayes, and Kasnitz 2010 — Disability Studies as Critical Inquiry (ref #99/100)**
*ASSETS '10*, 3–10. https://doi.org/10.1145/1878803.1878807
Calls for the AT/accessibility field to engage with disability studies scholarship (social model, crip theory). This paper triggered a methodological shift in how researchers frame disability in HCI — directly relevant to understanding the theoretical framing of any accessibility study.

**3. Lazar, Goldstein, and Taylor 2015 — Ensuring Digital Accessibility (ref #87/88)**
*Ensuring Digital Accessibility through Process and Policy*. Morgan Kaufmann.
The primary reference book for web/digital accessibility policy, law (Section 508, ADA), and implementation process. Cited as the foundational text on the policy side of digital accessibility.

**4. Grussenmeyer and Folmer 2017 — Accessible Touchscreen Survey (ref #52)**
*ACM Trans. Access. Comput.* 9, 2. https://doi.org/10.1145/3022701
Comprehensive survey of touchscreen accessibility technology for people with visual impairments. Key reference for the state of the art in mobile/touchscreen accessibility research.

**5. Trewin et al. 2020 — AI Fairness for People with Disabilities (ref #148)**
*ACM SIGACCESS Accessibility and Computing*, 125. https://doi.org/10.1145/3386296.3386297
Workshop paper establishing the research agenda for AI fairness specifically in the context of disability — an emerging and critical intersection as AI becomes embedded in assistive technology.

**6. Spiel et al. 2019 — Agency of Autistic Children in Technology Research (ref #140)**
*ACM Trans. Comput.-Hum. Interact.* 26, 6. https://doi.org/10.1145/3344919
Critical literature review examining how autistic children are positioned (or sidelined) as research participants. Directly relevant to participatory methods, research ethics with disabled participants, and cognitive accessibility evaluation methodology.

**7. Bennett, Brady, and Branham 2018 — Interdependence as a Frame (ref #16)**
*ASSETS '18*, 161–173. https://doi.org/10.1145/3234695.3236348
Proposes interdependence — rather than independence — as the appropriate design goal for assistive technology. Challenges the dominant independence-first framing and has influenced how researchers conceptualize AT design goals.

**8. Hofmann et al. 2020 — Living Disability Theory (ref #58)**
*ASSETS '20*, 1–13. https://doi.org/10.1145/3373625.3416996
Reflects on the gap between academic disability theory and lived disability experience in access, research design, and technology design. Important for anyone designing studies with disabled participants or framing accessibility claims.
