## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **IGDA-SIG [41] — Game Accessibility White Paper (2004)**: The 19-guideline document this paper critiques as limited and genre-specific. Essential for understanding the prior state of game accessibility guidelines and why Yuan's interaction-model approach improves on them.

2. **Grammenos et al. [34] — UA Chess (2005)**: Foundational paper on universally accessible game design for a board game — relevant for universal design principles and multi-impairment support in a single game.

3. **Simpson & Koester [80] — Adaptive one-switch row-column scanning (1999)**: Core AT paper on scanning mechanisms that underpin motor-accessible game design; relevant for any work on alternative input systems.

4. **Yuan & Folmer [106] — Blind Hero (2008)**: Direct application of this paper's strategy framework to build a haptic-feedback accessible music game; shows how the "Visual → Haptic" replacement strategy is implemented in practice.

5. **Folmer et al. [27] — Textsl (2009)**: Command-based virtual world interface for visually impaired users; extends this paper's accessible game strategies into virtual world environments and is relevant for VR/AR accessibility work.
