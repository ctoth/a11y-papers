Conway, Oppegaard, and Hayes (2020) present a three-year mixed-methods study with 69 stakeholders (blind, visually impaired, and sighted NPS staff) across 50+ National Park Service sites, producing empirically grounded best-practice guidelines for audio describing printed maps through five affinity clusters covering the need for map AD, describer guidance, boundary work, experimentation over prescription, and AD as community outreach. Key findings include a three-step description workflow (identify purpose, short description, long description with navigation guide), evidence that subject-matter expertise combined with AD training is essential for quality output, and demonstration that non-specialist park staff can produce useful map audio descriptions with proper guidelines through the open-source UniDescription Project platform. For this collection, the paper provides the practical production methodology for map audio description -- the approach that Biggs_2025_MapEquivalentPurpose independently evaluates as achieving 100% map equivalence -- while exemplifying the participatory research methodology with disabled co-researchers advocated by Mankoff_2010_DisabilityStudiesCriticalInquiry.
