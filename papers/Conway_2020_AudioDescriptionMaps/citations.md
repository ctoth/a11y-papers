## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Buzzi et al. (2011)** -- "Making visual maps accessible to the blind" -- Direct technical approach to map accessibility for blind users at UAHCI 2011; complements this paper's qualitative guidelines with a technical implementation perspective.

2. **Schinazi et al. (2016)** -- "Spatial navigation by congenitally blind individuals" -- Comprehensive cognitive science review of how blind individuals navigate space; extends Millar (1995) already in our collection with updated findings.

3. **Meloncon (2013)** -- *Rhetorical AccessAbility* -- Foundational edited volume connecting technical communication with disability studies; theoretical framework for this paper's approach.

4. **Thinus-Blanc & Gaunet (1997)** -- "Representation of space in blind persons" -- Psychological Bulletin review on spatial cognition without vision; directly relevant to understanding what blind users need from map descriptions.

5. **Stock et al. (1995)** -- "Mental representations of maps and verbal descriptions" -- Evidence that maps and verbal descriptions produce different mental representations; foundational for understanding why transmodal translation of maps is non-trivial.
