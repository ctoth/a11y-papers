# Citations

## Reference List

Gaver, W. W. (1994). Using and Creating Auditory Icons. In G. Kramer (Ed.), *Auditory display: Sonification, audification, and auditory interfaces* (pp. 417-446). Reading, USA: Addison-Wesley.

Mynatt, E. D. (1997). Transforming graphical interfaces into auditory interfaces for blind users. *Human-Computer Interaction, 12*, 7-45.

Winberg, F. & Hellström, S. O. (2000). Investigating Auditory Direct Manipulation: Sonifying the Towers of Hanoi. In *CHI 2000 Extended Abstracts*.

## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Gaver 1994** — "Using and Creating Auditory Icons" — The foundational reference for audio icon / earcon design. Westin's "3D sound icons" for game objects are a direct application of this concept. Relevant to hearing accessibility and non-visual interface design.

- **Mynatt 1997** — "Transforming graphical interfaces into auditory interfaces for blind users" — Directly describes the core challenge Terraformers solves: how to translate visual information into audio for blind users. Published in *Human-Computer Interaction*, a high-impact venue. High priority for this collection.

- **Winberg & Hellström 2000** — "Investigating Auditory Direct Manipulation: Sonifying the Towers of Hanoi" — Auditory manipulation research from CHI 2000, cited as related work on how spatial/direct audio interaction works for problem-solving tasks. Relevant to the research underpinning Terraformers' sonar/spatial audio design.
