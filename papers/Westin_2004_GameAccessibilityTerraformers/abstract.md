# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

Terraformers is the result of three years of practical research in developing a real-time 3D graphic game accessible for blind and low vision gamers as well as full sighted gamers. This presentation focus on the sound interface and how it relates to the 3D graphic world, and also include post mortem survey results from gamers and comments to those.

---

## Our Interpretation

This paper documents Terraformers, a real-time 3D game developed from 2000–2003 to be fully playable by blind and low-vision players alongside sighted players, addressing the gap between mainstream 3D games (vision-required) and audio-only games for blind players. The key finding is that a complete spatial audio interface — Sound Compass, Sonar, GPS, and voiced menus — can substitute for all visual navigation and inventory functions in a 3D game, as validated by a post-mortem survey of blind paying customers. For this collection, it is the earliest concrete implementation-level case study of game accessibility for visual impairment and serves as the origin point of the IGDA Game Accessibility SIG.
