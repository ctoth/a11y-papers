## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Brock et al. (2015)** - "Interactivity Improves Usability of Geographic Maps for Visually Impaired People" - The most rigorous empirical evaluation (24 blind participants) directly comparing interactive vs. non-interactive tactile maps. Essential for evidence on whether interactivity actually helps.

2. **Kane et al. (2011)** - "Access Overlays: Improving Non-Visual Access to Large Touch Screens for Blind Users" - Introduces Edge Projection, Neighborhood Browsing, and Touch-and-Speak interaction techniques. Directly relevant to designing accessible touch interfaces.

3. **O'Modhrain et al. (2015)** - "Designing Media for Visually-Impaired Users of Refreshable Touch Displays" - Design guidelines for the emerging refreshable display technology that this review identifies as theoretically ideal. IEEE Transactions on Haptics.

4. **Klatzky et al. (2014)** - "Touch-Screen Technology for the Dynamic Display of 2D Spatial Information Without Vision" - Cognitive science foundations for understanding how touch-based spatial access works for blind users. Important for justifying design decisions.

5. **Parkes (1988)** - "NOMAD: An audio-tactile tool" - The foundational prototype that launched the entire field of accessible interactive maps. Historical reference point for all subsequent work.
