## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Lo et al. (2020) — S2ORC** [24]: The Semantic Scholar Open Research Corpus is the foundational extraction infrastructure for SciA11y. Understanding S2ORC's capabilities and limitations directly explains SciA11y's extraction quality. Highly relevant for anyone building on top of structured scientific paper corpora.

2. **Lazar et al. (2017) — Making computing more inclusive** [23]: The prior PDF accessibility compliance study that Wang et al. replicate and extend. Provides historical baseline and established methodology for the 5-criteria assessment; essential companion to this paper.

3. **Siegel et al. (2018) — DeepFigures** [43]: The figure/table extraction component of SciA11y. Relevant for projects needing to automatically identify and extract visual content from scientific PDFs.

4. **Brady et al. (2015) — Accessible PDFs for conference proceedings** [7]: The earlier CHI/ASSETS compliance analysis. Establishes that community policy-driven compliance improvements are possible but limited; prior work directly discussed in Wang et al.'s Section 2.

5. **Ahmetovic et al. (2018) — Axessibility LaTeX package** [1]: Provides accessible mathematical equations from LaTeX source — the gap that SciA11y cannot fill. Directly addresses SciA11y's stated limitation of equation inaccessibility.
