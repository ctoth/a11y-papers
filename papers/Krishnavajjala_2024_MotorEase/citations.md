## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Salehnamadi et al. (2022) — Groundhog (ASE 2022):** Direct baseline for the expanding-section detector; the most relevant prior automated motor-accessibility tool. Essential for understanding the state of the art MotorEase improves upon.

2. **Alshayban et al. (2020) — Accessibility Issues in Android Apps (ICSE 2020):** Large-scale survey of Android accessibility issues; provides landscape context for what violations are most prevalent and which were previously studied.

3. **Kong et al. (2021) — Touch target sizing:** The empirical basis for the 8-pixel icon distance threshold used in MotorEase; following this citation gives the measurement methodology behind the threshold.

4. **Salehnamadi et al. (2021) — Latte (CHI 2021):** Replay-based accessibility testing framework; related infrastructure for automated accessibility evaluation that complements MotorEase's static analysis.

5. **Chiou et al. (2021/CHI 2023) — Keyboard accessibility failures:** Related automated detection of a different accessibility modality (keyboard-only navigation); methodologically parallel to MotorEase for a different interaction type.
