## Key Citations for Follow-up

**[29] Lundgard & Satyanarayan (2021)** — "Accessible visualization via natural language descriptions: A four-level model of semantic content." IEEE TVCG. The semantic framework directly built into FigurA11y's guidelines. Foundational for any data visualization accessibility work. High priority for this collection.

**[4] Chintalapati, Bragg & Wang (2022)** — "A dataset of alt texts from HCI publications." ASSETS 2022. Established the 4.6% valid alt text baseline that motivates FigurA11y. Direct empirical predecessor; would ground claims about alt text prevalence in computing venues.

**[46] Williams et al. (2022)** — "Toward supporting quality alt text in computing publications." W4A 2022. Author interview study and descriptiveness rubric. Directly cited as the basis for FigurA11y's design goals and quality assessment approach.

**[30] Mack et al. (2021)** — "Designing tools for high-quality alt text authoring." ASSETS 2021. Templates vs. auto-generation comparison (templates won). Key design predecessor; FigurA11y's improvement over this baseline is part of its contribution narrative.

**[48] Wu et al. (2017)** — "Automatic alt-text: Computer-generated image descriptions for blind users on a social network service." CSCW 2017. Facebook's large-scale automatic alt text system — the main industrial precedent for automated alt text generation; important for situating this work in the broader deployment landscape.
